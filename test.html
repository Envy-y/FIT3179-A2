Wagering Woes

A Data Dive into EGM Loss and Socioeconomic Impact

This visualisation poster aims to explore and highlight the economic and social impacts of losses from electronic gaming machines (EGMs) across all Victorian Local Government Areas (LGAs). By integrating data such as total losses, SEIFA scores, unemployment rates, and the number of EGMs per 1000 people, the poster provide insights into how gambling activities (notably loss from EMGs) are distributed across different LGAs, and their subsequent socialeconomic impacts. The visualisations in the poster seek to uncover patterns that can inform policy discussions and targeted interventions to mitigate the negative effects of EGMs on vulnerable communities.

Note: All data is for the financial year 2021-2022

Glossary

  • LGA: Local Government Area, a geographical area that is a division of a local government.
  • SEIFA Score: Socio-Economic Indexes for Areas Score, a measure of the relative socio-economic advantage and disadvantage of areas in Australia.
  • Country: Rural areas outside of Melbourne.
  • Metro: Metropolitan Areas within Melbourne.
  • EGM: Electronic Gaming Machines
