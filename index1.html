FIT3179 Homework - Interactive Chart

This parallel coordinate plot compares Population, Workforce, Gambling Loss, and SEIFA Score for different Victorian LGAs. The graph divides the two types of LGAs, Country and Metro into two different colours, red and blue respectively. Overall, it seems that Metro LGAs have higher Population, Workforce, Gambling Loss and SEIFA Score compared to Country LGAs, but that does not hold true for all. In fact, there is a clear negative correlation between Gambling Loss and SEIFA Score, so some Metro LGAs with high Gambling Loss end up with lower SEIFA Scores than most Country LGAs.
Key Terms:
SEIFA Score: Socio-Economic Indexes for Areas Score, a measure of the relative socio-economic advantage and disadvantage of areas in Australia.
LGA: Local Government Area, a geographical area that is a division of a local government.
Country: Rural areas outside of Melbourne.
Metro: Areas within Melbourne.

This bubble plot shows Unemployment Rate on the x-axis, Loss per Adult on the y-axis, and Population as the size of the bubble. Excluding the LGAs with 0 loss (No Gambling Venues), there is a clear positive correlation between Loss per Adult and Unemployment Rate. Additionally, despite the data are normalised, we see that LGAs with larger populations still tend to have higher Loss per Adult and Unemployment Rate.

Additionally, with the colour channel being used to encode the type of LGA, we can see that The loss per Adult is quite similar between Metro and Country LGAs, but Metro LGAs tend to have higher Unemployment Rates. This is likely due to the fact that Metro LGAs have higher populations and less job opportunities compared to Country LGAs.
